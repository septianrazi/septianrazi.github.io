Developed an interactive Cornell Box simulation in Three.js as a controlled environment for exploring lighting, materials, and rendering behaviour.

- Implemented a **physically-inspired scene setup** to experiment with light interaction, surface materials, and shadow behaviour
- Explored different **material and texture configurations** to understand their visual impact under varying lighting conditions
- Extended the project with **WebXR support**, enabling the scene to be experienced in immersive 3D environments
- Built as a lightweight, browser-based platform for rapid iteration and visual experimentation

- Used a canonical graphics benchmark to investigate **real-time rendering trade-offs in the browser**
- Bridged **traditional rendering concepts** with interactive and immersive web technologies

_Focused on understanding and experimenting with real-time lighting and material behaviour in interactive 3D environments._
