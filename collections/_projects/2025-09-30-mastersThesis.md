Designed and developed a Mixed Reality system to enhance the football spectator experience through immersive, interactive data visualisation.

- Integrated **live match data** (StatsBomb, Opta) into a real-time pipeline for spatial visualisation
- Built a **Mixed Reality front-end** allowing users to view and interact with football data in 3D alongside live or recorded matches
- Developed a **middleware tool for pundits and analysts**, enabling them to actively curate, highlight, and control how data is presented to viewers in real time
- Enabled analysts to construct **guided narratives and focal points**, shifting the experience from passive viewing to directed, insight-driven exploration
- Designed interaction techniques for exploring **player positioning, events, and tactical structures** in an immersive setting

- Introduced a **human-in-the-loop interaction model**, where experts shape the audience’s immersive experience rather than relying on fully automated visualisations
- Bridged the gap between **broadcast viewing and analytical tools**, enabling deeper engagement with the game
- Addressed challenges in **presenting dense, real-time data** without overwhelming users in immersive environments

Conducted user studies to evaluate usability and effectiveness of the system.

_Focused on transforming passive sports viewing into an interactive, data-driven immersive experience._
